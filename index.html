The Uniqueness of French Desserts

French desserts stand out for their exquisite appearance, diverse textures and flavors, as well as the craftsmanship and traditions that have been passed down through generations.

Learn More

Exquisite appearance

French desserts are known for their meticulously designed shapes and decorations, providing visual enjoyment.

Diverse textures & rich flavors

French desserts offer a variety of textures, they can be crisp, soft, or smooth, always accompanied by a wide range of flavors. They provide a rich, diverse gustatory experience.

Fine craftsmanship & unique tradition

French desserts chefs demonstrate skillful techniques, ensuring top-quality pastries while honoring the rich heritage of French pastry making.

Events

Share some festivals with you

Le Salon de la Pâtisserie

Paris

It's an annual event in Paris for pastry enthusiasts. It showcases French pastries and offers activities like masterclasses and competitions. It's a must-visit for pastry lovers in Paris.

Fête des Lumières

Lyon

Helded every December 8th. In addition to light shows and fireworks, people can also taste a variety of traditional French desserts, such as Mille-feuille and Profiteroles.

La Fête de la Galette

France

It is an annual celebration in France that celebrates the traditional pastry called "galette." It brings communities together to enjoy this delicious treat and embrace its cultural significance.

Desserts

The 3 Iconic Desserts

Macarons

Originated in Paris, France.

Made with egg whites, powdered sugar, almond flour, and food coloring, they have a crispy exterior, soft texture, and come in various colors. The flavor is a sweet combination of almond and sugar. They are often filled with cream or jam and enjoyed with coffee or tea.

Canelé

originated in Bordeaux, France.

It is made with a custard-like batter of flour, sugar, milk, and egg yolks, infused with vanilla and rum. Canelé has a caramelized and crispy exterior, while the inside is soft, custardy, and filled with rich vanilla flavors.

Madeleine

originated in Commercy, France.

It is made with flour, sugar, eggs, and butter. The distinctive shell-shaped cake has a soft and fluffy texture and is infused with a rich buttery flavor. Often enhanced with lemon zest or vanilla, Madeleines offer a delicate citrus or aromatic taste.
